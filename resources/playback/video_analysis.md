## 5. THE GAME HUD (Status Bar)

The status bar is **permanently fixed at the bottom of the screen** throughout all gameplay sections. It does NOT scroll with the level.

**Status bar composition:**
- Green vine/chain border running the full width
- **Left side:** A grotesque face tile (changes expression/colour over time — appears to show lives or status)
- **Centre:** A small square pixel icon (possibly the book/artifact the player is recovering)
- **Right side:** Another grotesque face tile
- Four skull decorations at the four corners of the bar
- The entire bar has a horror/gothic aesthetic with skulls, snakes/vines, and face imagery
- **Colour:** Predominantly green vines, purple/magenta inner panel, white skulls

**Health bar (Level 4 only, visible frames 0166–0188):**
- A horizontal bar appears in the **upper-right area of the screen** (not in the status bar)
- Three-segment bar: green segment (health remaining), cyan segment (partial), yellow segment (low health)
- A moon/orb graphic to the right of the bar
- This HUD element is unique to Level 4 — it is NOT visible in Levels 1–3

**Notable absence:** No visible score counter, no level number display, no explicit lives count beyond the face tiles in the status bar.

## 7. GAME MECHANICS OBSERVED

### Movement
- **Horizontal scrolling:** The game is a **side-scrolling action game** with continuous left-to-right progression. The camera appears to scroll smoothly as the player advances.
- **Running:** The player character runs at a consistent pace — no apparent speed modifier visible
- **Jumping:** Clearly visible in Level 4 — the character can perform standard jumps. Jumping also appears in the Skull Plains section (enemy jumps confirm jump physics exist).
- **Direction:** All observed movement is left to right. No backtracking observed.

### Combat
- **Ranged weapon:** The player uses a **spear, javelin, or blowgun** held horizontally. In Level 2 it appears to shoot at a distance; in Level 1 it is used as a thrusting melee weapon. The weapon animation shows the arm extending.
- **No sword or close-combat weapon change** is observed between levels — the same weapon is used throughout.
- **Enemy contact:** When enemies are near, the player thrusts/fires. No visible health loss animation on player (though the Level 4 health bar implies damage is tracked).

### Health System
- In Levels 1–3: No on-screen health bar. Either the player has many hits or health display is hidden.
- In Level 4: A three-segment colour bar (green/cyan/yellow) appears. It starts at roughly full and decreases.
- The "3. INFINITE ENERGY" menu option confirms a finite energy/health system exists.

### Lives System
- The face tiles in the status bar likely represent lives, but their change is not clearly observable in available frames.

### Level Transitions
- Between levels, a **black screen** appears for several frames (approximately 5–10 seconds of real time) — this is the loading/border wipe transition.
- The game HUD (status bar at bottom) remains constant across all level transitions.

### Platforming
- Level 4 is the primary platform-jumping level.
- The bridge has gaps the player must jump across.
- No multi-level platforms observed (all levels except Level 4 are single ground-plane sidescrollers).

### Collectibles / Objects
- A **square pixel object** in the centre of the status bar (visible from frame 0090 onward) appears to be a key collectible — possibly the book/artifact mentioned in the story.
- No individual pickup items (coins, gems, power-ups) are visible on screen during gameplay.

## 10. WHAT NEW LEVELS MUST LOOK/FEEL LIKE

To match the existing game's style, any new levels must follow these design principles:

### Visual Style Rules
1. **Single ground plane:** All existing levels (except Level 4) are flat single-platform side-scrollers. No multiple-floor platforming.
2. **Rich background art:** Each level has a distinctive, densely drawn background with multiple visual elements — not spare or empty. Backgrounds use 2–3 colour attribute blocks per character cell.
3. **ZX Spectrum colour constraints:** The game respects the 8x8 colour attribute grid. Monochrome rendering within colour blocks. No colour bleeding (achieved by keeping each sprite within colour boundaries or accepting clash on transparent backgrounds).
4. **Monochrome players and most enemies:** The player character and most enemies are rendered in white on their background colour — they do not have their own colours. This avoids colour clash.
5. **Colour-coded level identity:** Each level has a dominant background colour scheme:
   - Level 1: Yellow/grey/black (wasteland)
   - Level 2: Black/bright green/magenta (cave)
   - Level 3: Grey/yellow-gold/cyan (temple interior)
   - Skull Plains: Black/white (monochrome ruins)
   - Level 4: Black/magenta/cyan (night bridge)
6. **Status bar:** Always present at bottom. Any new level must preserve this 32-pixel-tall bar.

### Enemy Design Rules
1. Enemies are large sprites — they fill significant screen space (often 32×32 to 64×64 pixels equivalent)
2. All enemies walk/move horizontally toward the player
3. Some enemies have simple 2-frame animations (walk cycle)
4. No projectile-firing enemies observed — combat is melee/proximity-based
5. Enemy types escalate in size and visual complexity level by level

### Suggested New Level Themes (to complete the game)

**Level 5 — "KAMUIR'S FORTRESS" (Final Level)**
- Setting: Interior of Kamuir's stronghold, following the bridge crossing
- Visual palette: Red/orange/black — volcanic, hellish interior
- Background: Lava flows, iron chains, demonic architecture
- Enemies: Demon warriors, fire elementals, Kamuir's personal guards
- Must end with **boss encounter: KAMUIR** himself (as shown in intro — tall, muscular, flame headdress)
- Mechanic: Combat + platform elements, health bar (like Level 4) throughout

**Level 6 — "THE BOOK CHAMBER" (Boss Room)**
- Small final room/chamber
- Kamuir boss fight
- Book pickup after boss defeat
- Victory cutscene

### Mechanics for New Levels
- The health bar system should be retained from Level 4 for the final levels
- Jump mechanic (established in Level 4) should be more prominent in the fortress
- A "Run or Die!" style timed section could appear again as a recurring mechanic

## 11. KEY TECHNICAL OBSERVATIONS

- **Screen resolution:** 256×192 pixels (standard ZX Spectrum), displayed in the video with letter-boxing
- **Attribute grid:** 8×8 pixel colour cells, 32×24 grid
- **Colour use:** Bright colours (BRIGHT flag) used extensively — bright green, bright cyan, bright yellow, bright magenta
- **Scrolling implementation:** The background scrolls left-to-right; the status bar does NOT scroll (it is drawn separately each frame in the lower screen area)
- **Sprite size:** Player character approximately 16×24 pixels; enemies range from 16×16 (small) to 48×48 (large bosses)
- **Frame rate:** Appears to be approximately 12–25 fps (smooth enough for ZX Spectrum, likely using interrupt-driven animation)
- **Loading:** Black screens between levels suggest tape-loaded segments or page-swapped code/data
